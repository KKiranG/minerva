ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK:

1. COMMODITY REGIME for the stock's primary mineral
   - Current price vs 1-year average (% premium/discount)
   - Supply/demand balance: TIGHT, BALANCED, OVERSUPPLIED
   - Price trend direction over last 90 days

2. SUPPLY CHAIN POSITION
   - Where does this company sit? (mining/separation/processing/manufacturing)
   - How many competitors exist at this stage?
   - What is the company's differentiation?
   - Is there a structural advantage (only non-China source, only US processor, etc.)?

3. CHINA DEPENDENCY SCORING
   Score 0-10 based on:
   - What % of global supply of this mineral does China control?
   - Is the company dependent on Chinese feedstock or processing?
   - Would Chinese export restrictions directly impact this company?
   - Does the company BENEFIT from Chinese restrictions (competitive moat)?

4. CATALYST MATERIALITY CHECK
   - Is the catalyst actually relevant to this company's economics?
   - Or is it narrative-only (e.g., sector hype that doesn't translate to revenue)?
   - Example: A DOE grant for rare earth separation is material for UUUU if
     they're building separation capacity, but narrative-only if they're years away

5. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
   - Who are the direct competitors for the same mineral and same value chain stage?
   - How does this company compare on: stage, scale, funding, timeline?

WHAT YOU MUST NOT DO:
- Do not assume "critical minerals are important therefore this stock is good"
- Do not conflate sector tailwinds with company-specific advantages
- Do not ignore that most of these companies are years from meaningful revenue
- Do not treat all government attention as equal — materiality varies enormously
